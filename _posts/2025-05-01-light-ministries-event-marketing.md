## Overview

**Client:** Light Ministries  
**Location:** Lys De La Vallée, Sodnac Link Road, Quatre Bornes, Mauritius  
**Channels:** Facebook, WhatsApp  
**Scope:** Monthly event calendar, content planning, and social media management

Not every client is a global brand. Some of the most meaningful work I do is for community organisations that need clear, consistent communication to keep their congregation informed, engaged, and growing. Light Ministries is one such client — a faith community in Quatre Bornes with a regular programme of services, events, and special gatherings.

---

## The Brief

Light Ministries needed a reliable, professional social media presence that would:

- Keep existing members informed about upcoming services and events
- Invite new audiences into the community with welcoming, accessible messaging
- Reflect the organisation's values of transformation, healing, and fellowship
- Maintain a consistent visual and tonal identity across every post

---

## The Approach

### Structured Monthly Calendar

Each month begins with a comprehensive content plan. For May 2025, the schedule covered:

**Weekly Sunday Services**

| Date | Event | Speaker |
|---|---|---|
| 11 May | Sunday Service, 9AM | Bishop Kevin Permal |
| 18 May | Sunday Service, 9AM | Local pastor (in-house) |
| 25 May | Sunday Service + Parents Day Celebration, 9AM | Bishop Kevin Permal |

**Thursday Deliverance Meetings**

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| 8 May | Deliverance Meeting, 10AM |
| 15 May | Deliverance Meeting, 10AM |
| 22 May | Special dual session: 10AM + 7PM (with visiting Apostle Trevor Subramoney from South Africa) |
| 29 May | Deliverance Meeting, 10AM |

**Special Events**

- **Youth Night QB** — Friday 16 May, 7PM. Featuring worship, teaching, games, and discussion. Priced at Rs 100 per youth to cover costs.
- **Parents Day 2025** — Sunday 25 May, 9AM. A celebration of spiritual parents and family unity.

### Consistent Messaging Framework

Every post follows a consistent structure:

- **Clear event details** — date, time, location, speaker where applicable
- **Transformation-led messaging** — healing, breakthrough, freedom, and victory woven naturally into captions
- **An open invitation** — the recurring call-to-action, *"Bring a friend — don't come alone,"* reflects the community's welcoming nature
- **Warm visual identity** — photo static format with consistent headline structure and thoughtful emoji use

### Multi-Generational Reach

The May calendar intentionally addressed multiple audience segments:

- **Youth:** The Friday Youth Night spoke directly to a younger audience with a relevant, fun format
- **Parents:** The Parents Day celebration honoured a generation that forms the backbone of the community
- **Visitors:** The visiting speaker from South Africa added an element of novelty that often draws in those who don't usually attend

### WhatsApp Integration

In addition to Facebook, key announcements were adapted for WhatsApp, recognising that this remains one of the most direct and trusted communication channels for community organisations in Mauritius.

---

## Why This Work Matters

Community organisations often lack the resources — or the expertise — to communicate consistently and professionally. A well-planned content calendar means that no event goes unannounced, no service feels like an afterthought, and the community always feels informed and valued.

This engagement demonstrates that effective marketing isn't solely the preserve of big brands with large budgets. It's about understanding an audience, respecting their needs, and showing up consistently.
