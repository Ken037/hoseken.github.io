## Overview

**Campaign name:** MOOO-RIRE DE RIRE  
**Client:** La Vache qui rit (Bel Group)  
**Duration:** 15 June – 13 July 2023  
**Platform:** Facebook

This campaign marked a significant step in the brand's evolution in Mauritius — moving beyond product promotion into genuine emotional territory. The objective was to reassert laughter as La Vache qui rit's core brand value and begin the journey from a well-known brand to a truly *loved* brand.

---

## The Challenge

La Vache qui rit had been steadily building awareness and engagement in Mauritius, but the brand needed to deepen its emotional connection with audiences. Product-led content had done its job; it was now time to create experiences that people would associate with the brand's personality rather than simply its product range.

The campaign sat within a longer-term brand evolution arc:

> 2019: **Educate** → 2021: **Elevate** → 2023: **Live**

The 2023 chapter was about bringing the brand to life in a way that felt authentic and joyful.

---

## The Strategy

### Emotion First, Product Second

Rather than leading with the product, we led with an emotion — **laughter**. The campaign name, MOOO-RIRE DE RIRE (a play on "Moo" and the French for "laugh out loud"), set the tone immediately: irreverent, warm, and genuinely fun.

### Interactive Game Format

The centrepiece of the campaign was a series of three interactive videos hosted by **John Ducon**, a well-known Mauritian digital creator and comedian. Each video featured real participants from around the island playing games in front of a live audience, generating the kind of unscripted, genuine reactions that money cannot buy.

| Game | Format | Date |
|---|---|---|
| **Jeu de Mime** (Mime Game) | 2 players, 25-second limit | 25 June |
| **Pictionary** | 2 players, 45-second limit | 29 June |
| **Le Mime en Bouche** | Sound-only descriptions | 3 July |

Each game rewarded correct guesses with branded goodie bags, adding an aspirational layer to audience participation and encouraging viewers to engage, comment, and share.

### CSR Integration

The campaign was anchored by a genuine act of social responsibility. In partnership with **FoodWise**, La Vache qui rit donated food supplies to NGOs across Mauritius. This wasn't a footnote — it was woven into the campaign narrative with dedicated posts:

- **15 June:** CSR announcement — introducing the FoodWise partnership
- **9 July:** Collection and distribution video, showing the initiative in action
- **13 July:** Testimonials from the NGO recipients, closing the story with human impact

The messaging, *"Un petit geste, des grands sourires"* ("A small gesture, big smiles"), tied the CSR strand directly back to the brand's laughter-and-joy positioning.

---

## Results & Takeaways

The interactive format consistently outperformed standard content in terms of comments and shares. Featuring real, unscripted reactions — rather than polished promotional content — proved to be the most effective way to generate genuine word-of-mouth for a brand rooted in joy.

The CSR component added credibility and warmth to the campaign, demonstrating that La Vache qui rit's commitment to smiles extended beyond the product itself.
