## Overview

In June 2023, I led the social media campaign for **Axe** (Unilever) in Mauritius, promoting two key products: the Axe Roll-On Antiperspirant (48-hour protection) and the Axe Shower Gel. The brief was simple in concept but demanding in execution — bring the iconic "Axe Effect" to life across multiple platforms in a way that felt genuinely local, not like a copy-paste from a global template.

---

## The Challenge

Global FMCG campaigns are frequently built for international audiences and rely on the brand's existing equity to carry the message. In Mauritius, however, audiences respond far more positively to content that reflects their culture, humour, and language. The challenge was to honour the Axe brand identity whilst adapting it meaningfully for a local audience.

---

## The Approach

### Multi-Platform Coordination

I developed a coordinated content plan spanning **Instagram** (feed posts and Stories), **TikTok**, and **Facebook**, ensuring consistency in brand messaging whilst tailoring the format and tone to each platform's audience.

- **Instagram Feed:** Three animated posts highlighting product benefits, with captions blending English and Mauritian Creole to maximise cultural resonance.
- **Instagram Stories:** Four static stories published across the month (16th, 20th, 26th, and 30th June) to maintain a consistent brand presence.
- **Facebook:** Content mirrored from Instagram, adapted for the slightly older demographic active on that platform.

### Influencer Strategy

Rather than working with generic lifestyle influencers, I identified two local creators whose content style aligned naturally with the Axe brand personality:

- **Cedric Lebon (@capitaincasscouilles):** Featured in a TikTok video showcasing the Axe Shower Gel, incorporating a signature singing performance that resonated strongly with his audience.
- **Axel Louis (@axel_louis94):** Created two TikTok videos for the Axe Roll-On — one featuring POV acrobatic stunts, and another with a playful rose gesture moment — both leaning into the "confidence and attraction" narrative central to the Axe Effect.

### Budget Allocation

Each post was supported with targeted paid boosts ranging from **$50–$100**, ensuring the content reached beyond existing followers and into relevant new audiences.

---

## What This Campaign Demonstrated

- The ability to adapt global brand guidelines to a culturally specific market without losing brand integrity
- Strategic influencer selection based on audience fit rather than follower count alone
- Cross-platform coordination with consistent messaging and platform-appropriate formats
- Efficient budget management across organic and paid social
