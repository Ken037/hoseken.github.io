## Overview

**Client:** Durex (Reckitt)  
**Campaign Theme:** *Anou kozé* ("Let's talk" in Mauritian Creole)  
**Platform:** TikTok (supported by Facebook & Instagram)

Sexual well-being is a topic that brands in Mauritius have traditionally approached with caution — or avoided altogether. This campaign took the opposite approach: lean in, start the conversation, and do it in a way that is genuinely useful, culturally sensitive, and occasionally funny.

---

## The Challenge

Durex had an existing presence on Facebook and Instagram in Mauritius, but TikTok represented an untapped opportunity to reach younger audiences in a format they actively engage with. The challenge was twofold:

1. **Taboo subject matter** — Sexual well-being content requires careful handling to avoid being flagged, misread, or simply ignored by a conservative audience.
2. **New platform, new rules** — Building an audience on TikTok from scratch requires a different approach to content creation, algorithm strategy, and audience retention than traditional social media.

---

## The Strategy

### Pillars

The campaign was built on four content pillars that balanced education with accessibility:

| Pillar | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Taboo** | Normalise conversations around sexual wellness |
| **Well-being** | Connect the product to health and lifestyle |
| **Awareness** | Educate audiences on relevant topics |
| **Educate** | Position Durex as a trusted, knowledgeable brand |

### A Three-Phase Audience-Building Funnel

Rather than launching on TikTok in isolation, I designed a cross-platform funnel to give the account the best possible start:

1. **Phase 1 — Mini-campaign on Facebook & Instagram** to build awareness and prime the existing audience
2. **Phase 2 — Direct existing followers to the new TikTok account**, reducing the cold-start problem
3. **Phase 3 — Consistent posting cadence** to trigger the TikTok algorithm and expand organic reach through the For You page

### Content Creation Approach

The content deliberately avoided the polished, corporate feel that tends to underperform on TikTok. Instead, the direction was:

- **Short, easy-to-digest videos** — designed for a thumb-stopping moment in the first two seconds
- **Humour as a gateway** — comedy used to lower the audience's guard before delivering educational content
- **Roleplaying with props** — scenarios that feel relatable and genuine rather than scripted
- **Professional quality without looking produced** — clean visuals and good audio without the sterile feel of traditional advertising

### Algorithm-First Optimisation

Every video was optimised for discoverability:

- Compelling thumbnail with clear visual hook
- Strategic use of trending audio and effects
- Carefully crafted titles, captions, and hashtags
- Subject positioning and timing calibrated for maximum retention
- Active engagement strategy to boost early post performance

---

## Why This Matters

Campaigns like this require more than execution skill — they require judgement. Getting the tone wrong on a sensitive subject can damage a brand's credibility irreparably. Getting it right opens up a conversation that competitors are too cautious to have, establishing Durex as the trusted voice in a space where young Mauritians genuinely need credible information.
